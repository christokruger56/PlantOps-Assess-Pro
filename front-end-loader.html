Observation Checklist for Front End Loader Operation

1. Observation Checklist

Specific Outcome 1: Demonstrate Knowledge of the Functions of a Front End Loader

Assessment Criteria 1.1: The main function of the Front End Loader is explained in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Explain the primary purpose: To load, transport, and stockpile materials such as gravel or sand safely on construction or mining sites, using the front-mounted bucket.
  2. Identify key attachments: Standard bucket (e.g., 3.0 m³ capacity), forks for pallet handling; ensure quick coupler is locked to prevent detachment during lifts.
  3. Describe operational limits: Maximum dump height (e.g., 3m), lift capacity (e.g., 5 tons at ground level); reference load charts to avoid instability.
  4. Highlight safety integration: Functions must comply with site procedures to prevent spills or tip-overs during loading operations.
  5. Demonstrate on machine: Point to loader arms and bucket to illustrate loading cycle without engine running.

Assessment Criteria 1.2: Front End Loader operation procedures are listed, and the main functions of all major components are explained in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. List procedures: Pre-start inspection, controlled loading, safe travel, unloading, and shutdown; follow sequence to minimize risks like load shifts.
  2. Explain engine: Diesel-powered to drive hydraulics and wheels; check oil (e.g., SAE 15W-40) and filters to ensure reliable startup.
  3. Explain fuel system: Supplies diesel; inspect tank for contamination and secure cap to prevent spills during refueling.
  4. Explain hydraulic system: Operates lift arms and tilt via cylinders; monitor level (e.g., ISO VG 46) and pressure (200 bar) for smooth control.
  5. Explain electrical system: Powers indicators and starter; test battery (24V) and alternator output to avoid electrical faults.
  6. Explain braking/transmission: Hydrostatic drive for speed control; test service brakes and parking brake for immediate stopping.
  7. Explain cooling system: Radiator cools engine and hydraulics; check hoses for leaks and coolant level when cold.

Assessment Criteria 1.4: Maximum operating capacities of the Front End Loader are described in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Locate capacity plate: On ROPS cab or arm, indicating max load (e.g., 4.5 tons at 1.5m height) to prevent overload tipping.
  2. Describe bucket capacity: E.g., 2.5 m³ struck level for loose materials; derate by 20% for dense loads like rock.
  3. Explain lift charts: Account for machine tilt and reach; maintain center of gravity over front axle for stability.
  4. Verify on-site: Show reading for current setup, e.g., tires inflated to 3.5 bar for optimal traction.
  5. Note transport limits: Gross weight 15 tons; secure bucket lowered during towing on low-loader.

Specific Outcome 2: Start and Shut Down Front End Loader

Assessment Criteria 2.1: Pre-operational checks are carried out according to the daily inspection checklist.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Don PPE: Hard hat, high-vis vest, steel-toe boots, gloves; verify fit to protect against falling objects or pinch points.
  2. Walk-around inspection: Examine tires for cuts (min tread 10mm), hydraulic leaks, and bucket pins; check lights and mirrors.
  3. Engine area: Check oil, fuel, coolant levels; inspect air intake for dust and belts for cracks.
  4. Cab and controls: Test seat belt, horn, and gauges; ensure ROPS structure is intact for rollover protection.
  5. Undercarriage: Verify axle oil and steering linkages; complete and sign checklist, tag out if defects found.

Assessment Criteria 2.4: Start-up and shutdown procedures are followed according to manufacturer’s specifications and worksite procedures.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Enter cab, fasten seatbelt; clear area, sound horn before starting to alert nearby workers.
  2. Start engine: Key on, monitor warning lights, idle 3-5 minutes; test neutral lock and inching pedal.
  3. During run: Check EMS for faults, maintain low revs during warm-up; travel <15km/h on site roads.
  4. Shutdown: Park level, lower bucket fully, idle 3 minutes, engage parking brake, turn off key, remove it.
  5. Post-check: Apply wheel chocks, lock cab, report mileage/fuel; clean cab if contaminated.

Specific Outcome 3: Operate Front End Loader

Assessment Criteria 3.1: Front End Loader is operated safely, minimizing hazards in accordance with statutory requirements.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Site preparation: Inspect load area for stability, erect barriers; position machine facing load with clear escape route.
  2. Loading technique: Approach straight, tilt bucket level, lift slowly to 0.5m; avoid side-loading to prevent tip-over.
  3. Travel safety: Carry load low (0.3m ground clearance), 10m following distance; use spotter for blind spots.
  4. Hazard control: Activate beacon, avoid slopes >15° loaded; comply with MHSA for dust suppression and noise limits.
  5. Unloading: Dump at designated stockpile, back away slowly; check for spillage and clean up immediately.
  6. Emergency: If hydraulic failure, lower load gradually; activate kill switch if fire detected.

Assessment Criteria 3.7: Statutory requirements including relevant codes of practice and safety/health/environment legislation are listed and explained.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Obey road signs: Ensures compliance with OHS Act Section 8 for hazard identification and risk control.
  2. Use established roads: Aligns with ISO 14001 for environmental protection against erosion and pollution.
  3. Keep left when overtaking: Follows MHSA regulations for traffic flow and collision prevention on site.
  4. Adhere to speed limits (e.g., 10km/h): Reduces kinetic energy in accidents per code of practice for mobile equipment.
  5. Maintain 40m following distance: Allows reaction time as per safety standards to avoid rear-end impacts.
  6. Yield to loaded vehicles: Prioritizes stability of heavier loads under load management guidelines.

Specific Outcome 4: Document Front End Loader Use

Assessment Criteria 4.1: Front End Loader use is documented according to worksite procedures, including logs and reports.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Record operational hours: Log start/end times, loads handled (e.g., 20 tons gravel), and fuel consumption in shift book.
  2. Note defects: Document EMS warnings (e.g., low brake pressure), actions, and supervisor notification per maintenance protocol.
  3. Update service records: Sign daily checklist, track weekly grease points; retain for quality assurance audits.
  4. Report incidents: Detail near-misses (e.g., load spill), root cause, and preventive measures as required by OHS Act.
  5. Handover notes: Inform relief operator of status, e.g., "Bucket half-full, tires checked, no issues."
