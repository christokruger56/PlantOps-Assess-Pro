1. Observation Checklist

Specific Outcome 1: Demonstrate Knowledge of the Functions of a Grader

Assessment Criteria 1.1: The main function of the Grader is explained in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Explain primary purpose: To level and grade surfaces such as roads or earthworks by adjusting the blade for precise material spreading and compaction, ensuring smooth gradients for drainage.
  2. Identify key attachments: Moldboard (blade) for cutting (e.g., 4.2m width), circle for rotation, scarifier for breaking hard ground; verify hydraulic connections secure.
  3. Describe operational limits: Max blade side shift (e.g., 0.8m), articulation angle (25°); consult specs to avoid structural stress during turns.
  4. Emphasize safety: Functions integrate with site rules to prevent blade strikes or instability on slopes greater than 10%.
  5. Demonstrate visually: Point to blade controls and drawbar to show grading path without engine operation.

Assessment Criteria 1.2: Grader operation procedures are listed, and the main functions of all major components are explained in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. List procedures: Pre-inspection, blade setup, controlled passes, cleanup, shutdown; sequence to ensure even grading and minimal rework.
  2. Explain engine: Powers hydraulics and drive; check diesel oil (SAE 15W-40) and turbo for dust buildup to maintain power output.
  3. Explain fuel system: Tank capacity (e.g., 400L); inspect filters and lines for clogs to support extended operations.
  4. Explain hydraulic system: Controls blade lift/tilt (e.g., 200 bar pressure); monitor fluid (ISO VG 46) for leaks to prevent cylinder failure.
  5. Explain electrical system: Gauges and lights; test alternator (24V) and wiring harness for faults in dusty environments.
  6. Explain steering/articulation: Dual hydraulic for maneuverability; calibrate for precise turns without blade drift.
  7. Explain cooling system: Air-cooled or radiator; clean fins daily to avoid overheating during prolonged grading.

Assessment Criteria 1.3: Maximum operating capacities of the Grader are described in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Locate capacity plate: On cab frame, listing max drawbar pull (e.g., 15kN) to guide towing attachments safely.
  2. Describe blade specs: Cutting depth (0.3m), moldboard angle (45°); limit to soil type for efficiency.
  3. Explain performance charts: Gradeability (20% slope loaded); factor in ballast for stability.
  4. Verify setup: Adjust blade height per chart for current terrain, ensuring no exceedance of hydraulic limits.
  5. Transport notes: Axle load 10 tons; secure blade raised during haulage on trailer.

Specific Outcome 2: Start and Shut Down Grader

Assessment Criteria 2.1: Pre-operational checks are carried out according to the daily inspection checklist.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Wear PPE: Hard hat, high-vis, gloves, ear protection; inspect for integrity to safeguard against flying debris or noise.
  2. External walk-around: Check tires (pressure 4 bar), blade edges for wear, lights/mirrors; scan for fluid leaks.
  3. Engine bay: Verify oil/fuel/coolant; clean air filter, check belts and exhaust for blockages.
  4. Blade and hydraulics: Grease pivot points (NLGI 2), test circle rotation; ensure no binding.
  5. Cab: Adjust seat, test gauges/horn; confirm ROPS certification for rollover safety.
  6. Sign checklist: Note any Class A/B/C defects, tag machine if unsafe.

Assessment Criteria 2.2: Start-up and shutdown procedures are followed according to manufacturer’s specifications and worksite procedures.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Mount cab, secure belt; clear zone, activate beacon and sound horn to signal start.
  2. Key on, check pre-start warnings; crank engine, idle 5 minutes monitoring temps/pressures.
  3. Warm-up: Test steering/articulation at low RPM; verify blade response before full power.
  4. Shutdown: Park straight, raise blade, idle 5 minutes; engage parking brake, key off, exit.
  5. After: Chock wheels, lock up, log hours/fuel; report anomalies to supervisor.

Specific Outcome 3: Operate Grader

Assessment Criteria 3.1: Grader is operated safely, minimizing hazards in accordance with statutory requirements.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Site survey: Mark utilities, assess slope (max 15°), set cones; plan passes for even coverage.
  2. Blade setup: Angle for cut (30°), lower gradually; overlap passes by 0.3m to avoid ridges.
  3. Travel: Forward at 5km/h, use articulation for curves; maintain 40m distance from others.
  4. Hazard mitigation: Slow near edges, use spotter; comply with OHS Act for vibration exposure limits.
  5. Finish: Water for compaction, inspect grade with string line; cleanup windrows.
  6. Emergency: If loss of control, steer into bank; activate EMS shutdown if needed.

Assessment Criteria 3.5: Concerns during operation are identified and addressed (e.g., temperature increase).

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Monitor gauges: If temp rises >90°C, reduce load/throttle to cool; check for blockages.
  2. For EMS Class A: Stop immediately, shutdown per procedure, tag out machine.
  3. Class B: Note fault, continue if safe, schedule daily service repair.
  4. Class C: Alarm sounds, limit to weekly fix; report to maintenance log.
  5. Supervisor notify: Detail issue (e.g., "Hydraulic overheat"), actions taken.

Assessment Criteria 3.6: At least 10 rules for risk-free operation are listed.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Do not operate with open door unless belted: Prevents ejection in rollover per OHS standards.
  2. Stick to established roads: Minimizes environmental damage under ISO 14001.
  3. Keep left unless overtaking: Ensures consistent traffic flow per site rules.
  4. Obey speed limits (e.g., 8km/h): Protects machine life and control.
  5. Maintain 40m following distance: Allows stopping time to avoid collisions.
  6. Yield to laden trucks: Prioritizes heavier vehicles for safety.
  7. Avoid electric cables: Prevents shocks or shorts in wet conditions.
  8. Do not squash barricades: Maintains visibility and hazard markers.
  9. Watch for pedestrians: Halt immediately to prevent strikes.
  10. Wear seatbelt always: Complies with road act for restraint.

Specific Outcome 4: Document Grader Use

Assessment Criteria 4.1: Grader use is documented according to worksite procedures, including logs and reports.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Log metrics: Record hours, area graded (e.g., 500m²), fuel used in shift diary.
  2. Defect entry: Detail EMS codes (e.g., "Class B - low oil"), fixes attempted.
  3. Service update: Sign daily/weekly sheets, note grease intervals.
  4. Incident log: Describe events (e.g., near-miss with pedestrian), witnesses, actions.
  5. Handover: Verbal/written brief, e.g., "Blade sharpened, 200L fuel remaining."
